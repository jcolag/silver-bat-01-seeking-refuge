# The Silver Bat in the Springtime

I can count two important things that I've learned in my first few months at this.  First, as much training as I get, it looks like I may never feel comfortable out in the field.  Second, sending anybody into the field alone, even briefly, always creates a recipe for unnecessary pain somewhere.

And yet - a secret third thing - *sometimes* the experienced agents all sit at least an hour away, and children stolen from refugees by the Hepler administration will get auctioned off to fake foster families right in my neighborhood.  Therefore, all those nice, clean guidelines to keep everybody safe, and keep me from making a fool of myself, get thrown right out the window.

And I definitely can't pretend that it doesn't give me a visceral thrill to get out on my own, from time to time.  Between Wooster Amalgamated and the League, I rarely get to get around alone anymore.

"Are we ready, sir, or should I wait while you prepare a soliloquy to deliver to the city?"

For example, right now, I don't do this alone, because I have a snarky valet whispering in my ear.

"I'm scoping out the space, making sure that I have multiple ways out and a viable path for the kids, if anything goes south.  I have no interest in losing any kids."  Well, I wouldn't call that *completely* false.  After all, I do need to get this one completely right, even if the delay did come from my inner monologue.

I unroll my turtleneck to cover my face while putting on the glasses and beret.  Time to go in.  "Jeeves, cut power to the elevators and all the emergency doors, but keep an eye on the surveillance.  The *buyers* should be on their way and I don't want any of them making this any more complicated than it needs to be.  If you can find any evidence on them that we can pass on to the police, no matter how petty, so much the better."

"Yes, sir."

I swing down to the landing.  Based on the infrared scans through the wall, they have one guard posted in the room with the children.  I take pictures of everything through the window from my concealed position, sending them to the police, since I can't leave these kids to get distributed through the system and, without the kids, the police won't otherwise have any evidence to charge these jerks.  We'll worry about testimony later.

Sprinting, I manage to cover the walkway to the window without anybody noticing, and find the guard facing away.  I drill a tiny hole in the glass and push a small rod through to unlock the window.  Raising the window a small amount, I hit the guard with a tranquilizer dart.  Randomly drugging people doesn't always make for an ideal plan, but in this case, I don't have an easier way to disable the guard silently.

Climbing in, I ask the children to stay quiet, while I jam the door to the main room and spray acid into the doorknob to melt the lock into place.  They'll need to break it down to get in, buying us some time.

"OK, quiet as you can, and big kids carry the smaller kids, I need you to climb out this window and walk straight across to the window over the way with the scarf hanging out.  Can you do that?"  They agree, and I immediately start helping the kids over the windowsill.

Across the alley, I wave to Marco Quintero, one of my neighbors, a music teacher who I managed to save from a shakedown by agents of La Mano de la Serpiente.  They wanted him to teach his students a slightly different musical scale to not interfere with some sort of mass-hypnotic scheme they had planned, as if that sounds the slightest bit rational or likely to work.  In any case, Marco said he'd owe me a favor and, while I assume that he meant buying me lunch, at the moment, I actually need him to wrangle the kids down to Zarifa, down on one of the street levels, where she can take the kids back to the Arcology where we can find their parents.  And I feel relatively confident that he didn't recognize me as anything other than a masked vigilante.

I follow them across to the apartment that we borrowed for the evening, treating the usual residents to a night on the town.  "OK, kids, I'll meet back with you later, but you'll be safe now.  Just follow Mr. Marco, and he'll put you on a bus for a fun ride to someplace where we can find your families!"

One of the older kids hugs me.  "They tried to hurt us and told us we didn't have families anymore."  I suppose that crying would be a bad thing, right now.  It might stain the turtleneck.

"Well, we'll keep you safe, and we'll try hard to find your parents.  But first, you need to get to the bus, and I need to make sure that those men never hurt you again."  I hand Marco a small radio.  "A friend is watching on the building's surveillance.  If he sees anything in your way, he'll find you another route.  He'll also keep track of any little ones that might get sidetracked.  I'm sorry to put so much on you as a favor."

"Hey, no problem.  I can't really think of a better cause, and we're neighbors.  Oh, or am I not supposed to realize who you are?"

Damn.  "It wouldn't have been my *first* choice.  But as long as you know, and you're up for it, drop by for dinner one night, if you want to join the team."

"So, you do this a lot?"

I gesture around me.  "Well, it's not always *this*, and I usually find myself getting shot at a lot more than this, honestly.  Speaking of which, I need to go tie those creeps up before the police get here."

"Go.  We'll talk later."

I sprint back across the way and dive through the window.  The one guard here still sleeps soundly, but the others have started pounding on the door.  I cuff this one to the radiator to make sure that he'll stay out of the way, then set small explosive charges on the door's hinges and back out of sight.

The hinges blow, and after the smoke clears, four thugs rush in and head right for their buddy at the radiator.  I grab a chair and flatten one of these creeps with it, while kicking another's knee out from under him.  That should keep them out of my way for at least a few seconds.

The other two raise their rifles.  I dive in, grabbing the one on the left and throwing the attached guard into the other, causing a few shots to go wild into the ceiling.  I'll need to follow up there, to make sure that they didn't hurt anybody.

Then, moving as quickly as I can, I attack with the rifle butt, knocking the first one out, and pushing him into his friend again, while I spin around to clock the friend, too.

"The police have reached the elevators, sir," Jeeves points out.

"Let them up," I mutter.

I whirl around in time to see the remaining jerk struggle to his feet, rifle raised.  I kick him again and crack him over the head.  Poking my head into the other room to make sure that I didn't miss anybody and coming up empty, I take a minute to tie them together in the middle of the room.

Just as the door bursts open, I jump out the window and back up to the roof.  I don't exactly have the best timing, here, but they wouldn't have seen anything more than shoes, and they've got five people to book instead of me.  After checking on the apartments that might have sat in the line of fire of that bust of shots from the rifle, I crouch down and run across a few rooftops where I draped a rope, where I slide down to the road level and grab the sweater that I hid at the other end.  Ditching the glasses and hat, I can easily cross the quiet street to my waiting car.

"How did Marco get along, Jeeves?"

"We have all children accounted for on the bus, in transit to the Arcology, with only one minor mishap along the way, which will require some cleaning.  Zarifa reports that they all seem content, and she has handed out light snacks to hold them over."

"All right, excellent.  I'm on my way.  We're going to need all hands on deck to track down their parents.  Odds seem good that we won't get many full names and addresses, especially from the toddlers.  Any chance that we'll be able to incriminate the prospective buyers?"

"Possibly.  What I can only imagine might be the manager among the crowd that you confronted had his phone with him.  Our data recovery team found photographs of the would-be buyers on the device, along with pictures of the children and a list of prices."

"Well, that gives us something.  Hopefully, the District Attorney will find that enticing enough to investigate further."  And if we can find a couple of parents grateful enough to testify, that'll work out well.

My family likes to say that it quickly becomes important to celebrate the little victories, name every survivor, that it grows demoralizing to constantly focus on the scale of the work that we all need to do out there or, worse, to dwell on the failures, even though we memorialize them as well.  I have one small addition to that.  For my own personal sanity, after all the times that some creep has, stabbed, electrocuted, or burned me in the past few months, one who managed all three, I plan to celebrate the heck out of the simple fact that I managed to pull this off with surprisingly smooth sailing.  Even Marco, more or less a stranger to me and the whole organization, seems to have come through, which amazes me and probably him.

Speaking of celebrating the little things, that reminds me.  "Jeeves, do me a favor and call Yelena to ask if she can free herself up, this evening.  Then, order a pair of earrings for my Nana Isla's birthday.  Check the roster to see if she'll be in town any time that week.  See if I can schedule the usual monthly meeting with Waleed to see how close he has gotten.  Oh, and reach out for meetings with representatives that aren't blindly defending Hepler and his bigoted nonsense."

"Indeed, sir.  I shall also dust the books while I am at it.  Perhaps I will strap the duster to my elbow."

Wait.  Has he *not* been dusting the books for all these years?  Well, whatever.  I think what I really want to celebrate is that I might have actually found a way to make this bizarre lifestyle work, and maybe even do some real good along the way.

# Rallying Round the Old Threshold

I may never get used to using this space as my office.  Honestly, I may never get used to this as an office at all.  The hexagonal space dwarfs even my comically enormous desk, to start with, but more than that, they covered the walls in video screens.  In this case, I have dozens of maps, surveillance, dossiers, and profiles of agents, floating around my vision.  We might not have the capacity to go toe-to-toe with the United States government, but with President Hepler's cronies using him as a smokescreen, they've made themselves far more vulnerable.

Now I just need a team and a plan.  The time to buckle down has arrived...

* * *

"Welcome to Koralo Dareo International Airport," the attendant reiterates the banner hanging above his head, presumably not realizing or caring that the banner, the captain's announcement, the tickets, and probably a dozen other points, all where the airport or a worker there mentioned where I have arrived.  Such is life.

"Mister Wooster, over here!"  Ashraya, my iCare driver, recognizes me and waves me over, loads my bags into the trunk without a comment about the weight.  I feel too tired, and he seems like too nice a guy for me to refuse when he insists on showing me around the city of San Grobiano.  I guess that he probably needs the business, which I suppose doesn't surprise me, all things considered.

Listening to Ashraya, I quickly discover that, despite some architecture inspired by pre-Columbian structures and the assorted multicultural names, official retellings of the events and relationships have virtually erased the native populations of South Jefferson from history.  Narratives focus on brinksmanship between Americans and Mexicans, so much so that Ashraya shifts his "tour" to restaurants and theaters after I ask about the people who worked on the restored cotton plantations we pass on the outskirts of the city.

As expected, though, Flint Consolidated Services Corporation appears frequently on the tour.  "Old Isaac Flint," Ashraya explains in a well-rehearsed speech, "revolutionized the country's water infrastructure, starting around his South Jefferson home."  Actually, Flint tried to privatize and monopolize all access to water, and he had an uncomfortable number of discussions with his researchers about trying to monopolize access to oxygen, as well.  I also now know that some circles celebrate him as a general of GORGON.  I don't have the heart to correct Ashraya and tell him his role-model would happily have enslaved or killed everybody to make a few extra bucks, probably starting with Ashraya's ancestors, or that the state's largest employer probably hasn't grown any nicer in the last hundred years.  Sitting quietly through the tour pays off, though, as we pass several large facilities owned and controlled by subsidiaries, including Flint Correctional Services.

Soon after, checking into the Mammoth Hotel San Grobiano and receiving yet another redundant message welcoming to the city, I discover that Jeeves has booked me a suite, and that he has had the equipment shipped there and unpacked by local League members.  That probably means that they also deactivated any listening devices and set up countermeasures in the suite, but I still need to make a quick sweep of the suite, if only to make sure.

"Central," I use the old-fashioned throat microphone to minimize sound, as I set up some white noise emitters.  "Excellent work on the suite.  Patch me through to Pandora."

"Acknowledged, Reremouse.  Pandora online," he explains.  The codenames, in all honesty, feel like trying too hard, but this operation has a big enough footprint that I don't want to risk any explicit information getting out until long after we've packed up and left.

"This is Reremouse, Pandora.  Do we have a status update?"

"Target-Alpha will take the meeting with me.  I have already connected to their internal network and will enter the Consolidated building momentarily."

"Acknowledged, Pandora.  I'll stay on to monitor.  Signal if you need assistance."  She won't.  Pandora may just be a codename, but *pan*-*dora*, the all-gifted...well, they didn't choose it at random.

"Marwat Eadil to see Mister Flint," I hear over the link.

"Welcome to Flint Consolidated, Mrs. Eadil.  I have instructions to direct you to take the elevator to the thirty-fourth floor, and then you'll need to take the escalator the remaining two to the penthouse for security reasons."  I would accuse Flint of paranoid fantasies where he needs to see any potential enemies coming, but that mockery seems undermined by my adding yet a hidden microphone to the soundproofed collection that the previous sweep team left me.

Data flashes on the computer monitors that the advance team set up for me, as Eadil moves through the building:  Construction blueprints, organizational charts, chat logs, financial records, surveillance footage, more than a few photographs that'll probably turn into harassment lawsuits of various sorts.  It's probably too much to hope that *all* their security will have such superficial checks and countermeasures, but Pandora has cracked the legendary box open.

"You're late," Flint grumps, like he wants to audition for a role in a bad mob movie.

"Your watch is fast, if my punctuality is even relevant.  In this day and age, unsynchronized clocks cost the economy billions of dollars every year.  If your operation is this sloppy, Mister Flint, then it seems that we don't have anything further to discuss."  The clack of her heels echoes in Flint's office for a few seconds, before...

"My apologies.  It appears that you're right.  Please, have a seat and accept my apologies for relying on simple wound springs to judge people.  Also, it's Will; Mister Flint is my father, grandfather, great-grandfather, and so forth."

"Your time isn't as valuable as you think, Mister Flint.  Maybe don't judge people on punctuality at all.  Empty shibboleths like that seem ill-suited to the modern world."  She absolutely lives up to her reputation.  A college friend had a job interview with her, years ago.  He said that, a lot like the incident that I get to listen in on, she never said anything overtly offensive, and she didn't say anything she hasn't said a million times about herself in public, but she apparently comes off so remarkably forceful and charismatic that the mere hint of disapproval reduced my friend to tears.

"...Note taken.  Now, you wanted to talk about a possible merger," Will Flint replies, audibly deflated.

"Indeed.  As you know, my consortium includes several dozen startups across multiple verticals, pooling and leveraging data on the back-end for..."  They go back and forth about business things for quite some time.  I don't tune out, exactly, because I definitely need to focus on the mission, but the specifics of the conversation literally don't have any more bearing than normal corporate jargon to burn time in Flint's office.

Remember Pandora getting that name as the all-gifted one?  Not to mix metaphors, but as they say, beware of Greeks bearing gifts.  Because what Eadil *hasn't* mentioned in her portfolio of technology - probably because it has more use than yet another dating app for people who want a giraffe as a pet, and so Flint would absolutely want to acquire it - she invented a small gadget that can patch into a computer's input ports, allowing her confederate to more carefully hack into the CEO's computer than the network dragnet Eadil ran earlier.

Fāng Tè, the aforementioned confederate, is a hacker currently interning with Eadil's security company.  I *think* he has at least started making progress while Eadil pushes Flint around, but looking at the chat log, I...I'm not entirely sure what's going on.  He types some sort of stream of consciousness miasma of English, Chinese, Arabic, chat abbreviations, and emoji.  Phrases whiz past my screen such as, "أنا 📥 # 单据, 👀 💴 fraud w00t" go by.  And well, at least one of those words makes sense and looks important, and page after page of that sort of thing make an interesting contrast to the mergers and acquisitions speak that I get over audio.

On the other hand, after a couple of pages of watching this spew across the screen, I grow increasingly convinced that Fāng has guessed how long that he can do this before I break, especially when lines suddenly start running from the right side of the screen.

Now he has something in the chat about a kangaroo and footprints...jumping to another computer, maybe?  Following a trail?  I suddenly spot the name Euryale zip by on the chat, one of the names of the three mythological Gorgons.  Fāng must have gotten access to Flint's GORGON files.  "It looks like ENIAC has breached Euryale.  Central, you'll receive a variety of ledgers now that accountants should get to work on decoding, in case they have something beyond their normal operations in there.  Pandora, download progresses nicely, so keep Target-Alpha in your sights.  ENIAC, signal when you find the target."

* * *

Twenty percent.

"Nothing like this merger will work, without enhancements to the existing privacy policy.  They have all accepted agreements to allow their data to be shared between services under my umbrella, but sharing them outside the company is impossible."  Eadil is playing this out thoroughly, even though she has no intention of selling at the end of this.

"Fair enough, though we wouldn't be able to promise such a thing in perpetuity, perhaps for two years, no longer.  I can have my lawyers put together a preliminary draft for review while we discuss this.  You don't need to object that we haven't made any decisions.  If we don't complete this merger, I'll acquire some similar organization eventually, and would require something similar."  Flint has spent this time trying desperately to keep up, willing to tie up his legal department on the mere hint of potential market consolidation.

"It might be interesting, at that, to see what your team comes up with."

I obviously don't see Flint send the message to his legal team, since I only have the audio, but the message comes up on one of the screens.  "Pandora, you might want to know that Alpha's message includes explicit direction for his legal team to deceive on the privacy issue.  It might give you some leverage to capitalizing on when the draft arrives."

The conversation changes direction for a while, and...

* * *

Forty percent.

"Security is important, of course, but we're not convinced that the investment is warranted by such a cost sink."  Irony, thy name is Flint, I guess.

"Our experience, by contrast, tells us that even the rumor of a breach is enough to warrant skepticism with respect to the service's utility.  After all," Eadil emphasizes, "such incidents bring risks of exposure, integrity problems, the inconvenience of..."

It increasingly sounds like she can bulldoze this jerk all afternoon.  And I love having it in my ear, as she draws him into ambush after ambush, making him feel like he has the upper-hand momentarily, so that she can put him in his place again, and he falls for it every time.  Grant me the confidence of a mediocre white man, as they say.

* * *

Sixty percent.  I spot an American flag emoji as it scrolls through the chat log, suggesting that Fāng has located files for stateside operations.

"...would not be comfortable, given the reliance our users have on these services, without a guaranteed median response time."

"If users are as reliant on the services as you say, would they not be willing to wait a little bit longer?"

"We do track usage during congested periods and can easily see that there is a precipitous drop, some users never returning."

"Well, if that's the case, we'll want to see that data to work out what the most economical response time looks like.  We have a duty to maximize shareholder value, as you know."

"My people will supply that."

* * *

Eighty percent.  Wooster Amalgamated lawyers have summarized the choice bits from the privacy agreement for Eadil, giving her ammunition to push things further.

"...impossible to even consider allowing such a flagrant dismissal of a user's right to privacy, and the alleged timeline is entirely undercut by this clause down in subsection..."

During Eadil's tirade, I hear Flint actually gasp.  He doesn't realize it, but he now needs to face off against both Eadil and an invisible battalion of lawyers, so it looks like his sparring partner has spontaneously absorbed the entire draft contract, and even he has started to realize that he doesn't have a chance in this negotiation.  I really wish that we could've figured out how to smuggle a camera in there.

* * *

Ninety percent.  Almost there.  Fāng posts a series of building emoji, indicating that we've moved on to real estate deals and allocation.  I forward some of these numbers to Eun-Ju, because some of the residential data looks like we could plausibly find some pretext to swoop in on some of these and create additional community land trusts to fend off Flint and their ilk.

"...and yet, if we pay for each account at the time of the merger, we would have no idea which accounts have been created by real people or are at all active."

"Mister Flint...*Will*, the only reasonable measure of the strength of the network is the number of accounts, to which you have already agreed.  Therefore, it must stand to reason that each account carries a value, whether your team has the interest in authenticating anything they see or they do not.  Your lack of diligence does not decrease the value of the property."  Wow, she has really started putting the screws to him, now that we've gotten towards the end of this little excursion, even dripping out his name like a slur, almost certainly laying the groundwork for scuttling the deal, once she no longer needs to tolerate the idea.

"But we would need to invest in..."

I don't know if I can take much more of Flint's rants about not wanting to spend money, as if somebody put him on an austerity budget.  To my relief, we almost have everything that we need, here.

* * *

Ninety-nine...and one hundred percent, and true to form, Fāng floods the chat with a checkered flag.

"Pandora, we are clear.  Disengage at your pleasure."

"Mister Flint, it's becoming increasingly clear this isn't going to work.  Your team has only the most grudging interest in security or privacy, and even less in making this potential relationship worth anything resembling my time."  Well, that didn't take long.  I would've put good money on her going another couple of rounds with him just to put him in his place.  "As-salāmu ʿalaykum, sir."

"Pandora, ENIAC, thank you very much for your help.  We can comfortably put stage one to bed."  I only wish that it got easier from here.

# The Unbidden Guests

We pull the door open and stare into darkness, voices screaming in the distance.

Tè beams at us.  "I figured we'd have an easier go of things if they couldn't see us and couldn't set off any alarms."  That does sound fairly likely.

We pass through and pull the vault door closed behind us, giving the feeling that it traps us in a claustrophobic, inky blackness until we can get at our night-vision glasses.  We start to split up, when Tè stops us for another surprise.  "Two, incoming from the left," he whispers, just as a flashlight beam sweeps an intersection.  He holds up his tablet, showing GORGON and Fiery Cross icons overlaid on the floor plan, then pulls out four more tablets with the same map for each of us.  He then points to a cluster of a few of the icons at the bottom of the screen.  "The rings have tracking devices in them, which we can follow, here.  Inside their Faraday cage, we don't have signals scrambled, so they still work."

At the realization that radio will work, I motion to have everyone switch over to the throat microphones, to minimize the chance of anyone overhearing our communications.  "Can you change the signal on the five rings that we grabbed from those clowns, so that we can tell the difference?"

"Each signal is unique, boss.  I can tweak my tracking code and..."  The five icons at the bottom show up as the League of the Silver Bat's insignia.  "Better to change our presentation, I think, than make a change to the signal that someone else might notice."

"Fair point, Tè.  Got any more little surprises in store for us?"

"No, boss.  Well, no surprises for *now*, at least.  I'm working on something for the way out.  We'll see."

"Anybody else?  No?  Let's get right down to work, then.  We'll meet back here in thirty minutes.  Good luck."

With that, we head off on our assigned tasks.  We planned this assuming that we wouldn't have any ability to communicate, see the movement of our adversaries, or hide, so this might go more or less by the books, now that we have those advantages.

* * *

As I walk along the perimeter of GORGON's secure detention block, I come up behind two guards trying to patrol with flashlights.  Since they can't see me, I decide to have a some fun with them; a bit of paranoia on their parts will only help our situation, after all.  I creep closer, closer, close enough to reach out and tap the one on the left on the upper arm.  And I do exactly that.

"What," Left asks, turning to his partner.

"*What* what?"

"You just grabbed my arm."

"You're damaged.  Bad enough we have to deal with the lights, but you need to start seeing ghosts, too?"

I slip back over to them, slowly pushing in close again, creeping in and reaching out to flick the guard on the right's tricep.

"Very mature, jackass."  Right seems almost pleased for the excuse to lash back out against his partner.

"What are you on about?"

"Oh, right.  You fantasize that I hit you and denied it, so now you want me to see how it feels?  Is that it?"

"I did no such thing, you nut.  Take your own advice.  Do your job."

"Whatever."

Well, getting these two into a fist fight would certainly make it easier to get past them.  I give them another minute to let their attention lapse again, then creep towards them again.  Closer.

Closer...

Closer...

I reach out slowly, and...

"Hey!"  Guard-on-the-Right pivots on his heel and throws a punch at me.  Left spins, shining his light at me, blinding me for a second while Right dives at my mid-section.  I go down hard.  Damn!

I kick out, buying myself a couple of millimeters, but not more than that.  I use that to get another kick in to rotate away.  "Hawkshaw, Houdini, I could use a little help."  They should be the closest, not that I really have the time to pull out the tablet to double-check that instinct.

Righty grabs me again, just as I start to lift myself back up.  I take a jab to the gut.  Another.  I get a hold of his right arm and twist.  As he lurches back, I return the favor of his jabs and sweep his feet to push him off balance.  I rise back up to my feet in time for Lefty to rush me, but he moves far enough away that I can grab him and use his momentum to cause him to take a tumble.

Unfortunately, Righty has also gotten back on his feet.  He pulls a knife.  "Stand down, intruder.  If I need to forcefully subdue you, I'll just kill you."  He points his flashlight at my hands.  "Hell, I may just kill you just to *save* a mongrel such as you."  A small part of me admittedly half-wants to feel shock and dismay that he's a bigot, hoping that some of them don't see the effects of the policies that they support, but...well, let's just say that GORGON didn't exactly side with the Allies during World War II, and they haven't updated their policies much in the intervening decades; the Clan of the Fiery Cross has held a grudge since they the government informed them that they couldn't buy, sell, and breed people like cattle anymore.

I switch on my voice scrambler.  "Oh, give it up.  Your side not only lost the big war, but keeps trying to stage a come-back tour only to lose again, every last time."  If he wants to make this melodramatic, maybe he'll pay some of his limited attention to a little posturing of my own.  "You're on the wrong side of wars, on the wrong side of history, and on the wrong side of this fight.  But your side likes to lose, doesn't it?  Play to your strengths."

He snarls as he swings the knife.  I jump out of the way.  Swing.  Jump.  Swing.  Jump.  Swing.  Drop and kick.  His knees fail and I get back up.  He swings from his prone position before I can disarm him, slashing my pant leg.  Maybe my leg, too, but I don't have any time to check.

He thrusts again, as I close in, this time definitely digging into my leg.  As I scream, I discover that Lefty has gotten back onto his feet as he rushes me and slams me into a wall, knocking the wind out of me.

Shaking that off, I push back and wait for Lefty to rush me again.  He does.  This time, I grab him as he comes near and throw him into the wall.  The leg hurts like Hell, though, and I scream at the extra weight, again when Lefty kidney-punches me as he loses consciousness and collapses.

I might not lag far behind, honestly, but Righty has stood back up.  Then, I realize how ignorantly dismissive I acted about learning to fight and bringing weapons, thinking my teenage self-defense classes would be fine, and skipping out on lessons with the family over the last few weeks so that I could plan this mission.  I still don't want to hurt these dolts, even if they do live for their bigotry, literally looking to undermine everything the nation and really humanity stands for.  But a non-lethal way to take these two down would've gotten them out of the way without causing them nearly as much pain, let alone risking exposing League operations.

I lash out, diving for the knife.  I probably can't stop him completely, at this point, but I can at least make it harder to gut me.  Somehow, I manage to connect, grabbing and pulling down is arm.  I kick him in the ribs, screaming again as my other leg throbs.  I slam his arm into the wall once, twice, a third time and he finally drops the knife.  Another kick and this time the knife goes skittering into the darkness down the hallway.

He kicks at my leg, now.  I scream in pain again as I fall.  I grab onto his leg and twist, hoping to keep him down for a while.  He lets out a yelp, but keeps at me, getting a grip on my tunic.

Footsteps, off in the distance, getting closer.  Finally.

I don't think I can hold out much longer like this and don't even remember how long ago I called for Alexia and Bill to help.

"Lin-þí alsukj in þy staṉd̎!"  Ugh.  GORGON's guttural patois.  It's not enough for them to run around as a fascist fan club with delusions of grandeur, on their fifteenth or sixteenth Reich probably in just the last decade, but they have to have their own fake-language to exclude people outside their group, too.  Alexia and Bill didn't get here first.  That's not good.  That's not good at all.

"Þau lasð!"  I punch my dance partner in the jaw, hoping that he won't make the situation worse than it has already gotten.  I daze him, but can't knock him out.

Then, I hear two guns cock.  "Þau lasð," I try, and almost certainly fail, to reproduce the sounds to maybe buy myself some goodwill before they gun me down.  I pull my hands out of view and bow my head, limiting their view of my darker skin.  I'd say that I should have learned the language, but nobody really teaches courses in secret languages used by international terrorist organizations.  Wait.  There was *one* word I saw on the files.  "Ferg-me!"  With any luck, the pronunciation is close, otherwise...

"Aheve þy fœlms, fœrsvy, œr við muð pull þí daun!"

That doesn't seem like the best sign.  So much for luck.

Out of the corner of my eye, I see the flashlight of the guard I just managed to stop.  The fight or the fall damaged it and caused it to go out, but sits right at my fingertips.  They won't see it in their own weak light.  It looks like just about the only chance that I'll have.  I palm the light.  Stand up.  I feel wobbly, but I get to my feet.  The guards hold their rifles on me.

I fake a stumble.  Really, I only need to half-fake it, but I do need it to happen.  They lower their rifles slightly.  I don't pose any threat, after all.  Except that, with some luck, I might pose one.  I need to pose a threat, at this point.

In hopes of startling them, I take the flashlight and fling it at one of the guns while I jump for the other, surprising both of the guards.  I pull on the nearer rifle, bringing the guard towards me, and throw him over my shoulder.  He loses his grip, but not soon enough.  I can't get my arms into in a position to defend myself when, the butt of the other rifle thrusts into my chest.

I stumble again, this time not intentionally.  However, I can get my arms into the ideal position to bring my new rifle down on her arm, knocking her own weapon from her hand.  She kicks out in response, flattening me against the wall and dives for her gun, grabbing it and aiming it right at me.  I'd have a chance if I could focus, but I might have lost too much blood or taken too many blows to even see clearly.

I lose my footing and pretty much just wait to die, waiting for the sound of the rifle.

Yet the sound that follows isn't a gunshot.  It's the clang of metal on metal.  A knife flies out of the darkness, catching the guard in the hand, forcing her to drop the rifle.  Two - I sincerely hope that it's two and not my blurring double vision - more knives flash through the air, hitting her in the arm.  The cavalry has arrived.  Alexia, Lexi Magnus when she performs on stage, must have thrown the knife.

Indeed, in a lucid moment, I see Alexia rushing in and begins fighting with the remaining guard.  She moves quickly and efficiently, as befits a magician, I guess.

I stumble away from the wall, somehow thinking I might conceivably help in my state, but one of the guards who I thought that I knocked out has gotten back up in my haze, slipping behind me and pulling me into a choke-hold.  I try to throw myself down, hoping that I can take the guard down with me, but he already prepared for my flailing.  Trying to fight back, and even thinking that I might have gotten a couple of good shots in, I can feel myself losing consciousness.

Just as I think that I've gotten to the verge of blacking out, though, I hear a thumping sound behind me and a groan.  When my eyes clear, and probably more honestly, probably after I wake back up, Bill has started patching up my leg.  "I don't think this is too bad.  Lost some blood, but it probably took you off guard more than it put you in any kind of danger, unless you lost a bunch more blood somewhere else.  Good chance of infection, though, if we don't get that treated as soon as we get back."

"Thanks."  I never noticed his drawl, before.  He must feel shaken, too, if it started coming out.

"Thank us after we've made it back home.  Between now and then, we still need to pull this off, and we should also clean up this blood, or at least degrade the DNA, so your identity doesn't suddenly end up in the tabloids.  Oh, and eat this."  He hands me some sort of health bar that I'd probably never touch under normal circumstances.  "It should help with electrolytes and blood sugar.  And you'll need water, too."

I nibble, unsure how my stomach will react.  "Fair point.  Let's get back to work."  I get back up on my feet.  I waver quite a bit, and my leg hurts, but I haven't collapsed or screamed.  That feels like something.

Bill hands me another bottle.  "This is a mild acid.  It should degrade the DNA in your little puddles of blood past the point where GORGON will be able to use it to get any information on you.  It's probably easier than cleaning it up.  Houdini and I will secure the four guards."  Alexia already started on that, I notice.

I check the tablet.  We can see about twenty more guards posted around this facility, too many take the time to try and sideline them to get those obstacles out of our way, tempting as that suddenly seems.  The next shift should only lie an hour or so away, too, making that counter-productive, since another bunch would probably show up as we wrap up this group.  We'll need to work around them as originally planned.  I should've stuck to that plan in the first place, really, instead of provoking the guards who I found.

Before splitting up again, Bill gives Alexia a boost up to the ceiling.  She kicks through the drop ceiling and vanishes for a couple of seconds.  I hear some crashing around before she comes back out.  She drops back down to the floor and presents me with some sort of metal railing, a few feet long.  "You're going to want a cane or a walking stick to keep your weight off that leg, especially with that makeshift splint."

"Probably a good idea.  And it'll probably work out substantially better, if I get into trouble again, than trying to punch people.  Because that *clearly* did not work out the first time."

"Yeah, try not to get in any more fights.  You are...not good at that."  Oh, they noticed.

"In my defense, I didn't think I would actually get involved in that fight.  The four-on-one aspect took me by surprise, too.  But also, yes, I ditched a lot of training.  All right.  Let's go."

They each head off in opposite directions, back to their previous positions.  Heck of a little diversion, and a lot more of the schedule probably needs us to push it because I needed to feel clever.

Steadier now, I continue back along my route.  Step by step down the dark hall, I realize that the four guards behind me probably accounted for everybody covering this wing.  Then finally, I find my first target, the records room.

Of course, unlike Flint Consolidated proper, GORGON hasn't kept physical records since the late 1990s.  They've computerized everything now, which would make this a lot easier with...well, electricity.

"Hacker, fiat lux," I mutter into the microphone.

As I hear "gotcha, Honcho," in my ear, the computer starts booting up.  I pull the control dongle out of my pack and plug it into the back of the machine.  Once I have the computer running, I copy all the files.  Then, I activate Tè's little extra surprise.  The dongle lights up and, so he told me, it instructs the computer to delete all the files, then to propagate the deletion command through the backup system when that comes back online.  That won't give us a complete victory, but it means that this facility should no longer have much use to GORGON after we leave.

"Hacker, we're clear.  You can pull the plug."  Tè acknowledges, and the power goes back out, here.

As I re-pack the dongle and grab my walking stick, my heart sinks as I hear the door unlatch.  I glance at my tablet and don't see any of my team there.  I should've kept some attention on the guard movements.

As quietly as I can manage, I duck down behind one of the desks near the middle of the room, hoping that I can escape without another fight and gripping the walking stick tightly in case I can't do that.  The door opens slowly and silently, the GORGON agent obviously planning to catch someone by surprise.

The agent whispers something incomprehensible in that GORGON patois, though even with my limited ability to parse that mess, I feel pretty sure he addressed it to somebody named Cicilia, probably the guard whose ring I took.  That puts each of us in danger, me most directly.

The agent calls out for Cicilia, again.  I can see him looking at a tablet of his own, walking towards me.  I consider backing away, but realize that gives away the presence of a person.  Meanwhile, he gets closer, ever closer.  He reaches the desk where I've hidden and calls out one more time.

It feels like a risky move, but I remove the ring and roll further back into the dark room.  He jumps over the desk, looking to save his friend.  "Cicilia!?"  He sweeps his flashlight around the room, then again, more systematically.  Well, that dashes my hopes of him finding the ring and wandering off to look elsewhere.

It looks like he wants to brute force this job.  He walks to the other end of the room, four desks from me, circles it, and looks underneath.  He pulls out his rifle, almost seeming like it leads him instead of him using it as a tool.  As he searches the third desk down, I do some back-of-the-envelope guesswork about lines of sight.  It pretty surely looks like the safest way - the only way - to do this correlates highly the absolute stupidest way.

He makes it to two desks away, now, using his flashlight to sweep under the desks, which will potentially make this much more difficult.

One desk away, now.  I continue to notice that this doesn't feel like a particularly good or even rational plan, but all the better plans require me not squatting in this room alone with the guard.  Glancing down, I check the tablet again, to make sure this won't immediately get significantly worse and...it might.  I see one GORGON guard heading this way, though slow enough that their movement probably didn't start in response to my guy in here.

He reaches my desk.  I slip behind it to get out of his line of sight and, when he peeks underneath, throw a pen behind him, hoping for a brief distraction so that I can crawl to the next row of desks toward the door.  Another pen tossed into the corner as I make a break for the next row of desks.  This time, he shoots near where it lands.  While it distracts him, I do some quick and *ideally* quiet rewiring that I hope will work to my advantage.  One more pen flung to the opposite corner and I make it to the desk closest to the door.  I slip out of my hiding place for the final rush to the door and - 

"Hvæð isð þis?"

Damn.  I took much too long in there.  Still, I do have half of a backup plan.  I kick with my remaining good leg, catching the guard in the shins.  He cries out, which has exactly the effect I wanted, for once.  The guard inside the records room panics, letting off a burst of gunfire and rushing to the door, bringing him up against the cables I ran across the other's likeliest route out of the room.  We both see him topple over and, when in new guard's surprise, I bash him with the walking stick.  I tie the two of them up inside the room, grab all three rings as well as one of the rifles that I hope that I won't need to use, and close the door as I leave.

The rings, I throw down the hall in the direction I came.  If I get particularly lucky, anybody else looking for this Cicilia will follow that trajectory instead of me.

That still throws another delay into my schedule, bringing us precariously close to the deadline, and that falls almost entirely on my shoulders.
